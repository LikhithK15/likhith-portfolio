About

I write things
worth watching.

"The story is always inside the material. My job is to find it — and make the audience feel it before they understand it."

I'm a freelance Investigative Documentary Scriptwriter — not a content writer, not a generic YouTube scriptwriter. My work sits at the intersection of deep research, systems thinking, and retention-first narrative structure.

I've covered Sri Lanka's debt crisis, a ₹590 crore banking fraud, India's LPG supply chain vulnerability, semiconductor geopolitics, and the racial anxiety behind one of America's fastest-changing suburbs. If there's a system failing, a power shifting, or a story hiding inside a government report — I'll find the angle that makes someone stay.

Alongside documentary work, I write personal essay scripts for creators building real audiences around real stories — confessional, specific, and deliberately unpolished where polish would cost authenticity.

How I Work

Writing that performs.

01
Research First
I read government reports, academic papers, and financial filings — not just Wikipedia. Every claim is traceable. The script's authority comes from the research, not the narration.
02
Thesis Before Draft
A documentary isn't a summary of a topic. It's an argument about one. Before writing a word, I identify the central tension — the thing that makes the audience feel the stakes personally.
03
Retention-Led Structure
Good hooks. Escalating stakes. Deliberate pacing. Every section earns the next. The goal is a script where the editor has to fight the urge to keep watching while cutting.
