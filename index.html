About

"The story is always inside the material. My job is to find it — and make the audience feel it before they understand it."

I'm a freelance documentary scriptwriter specialising in investigative long-form video. My work sits at the intersection of primary research, systems thinking, and narrative structure built for retention — not just comprehension.

I've written on Sri Lanka's debt crisis, a ₹590 crore banking fraud, India's LPG supply chain, semiconductor geopolitics, and the racial anxiety behind one of America's fastest-growing suburbs. If there's a system failing, a power shifting, or a real story buried in a government report — I find the angle that makes someone stay.

I also write personal essay scripts for creators building audiences around lived experience — confessional, specific, and deliberately unpolished where polish would cost authenticity.

How I Work
01
Research before everything
Government reports, academic papers, financial filings — not just Wikipedia. Every claim is traceable. The script's authority comes from the research, not the narration style.
02
Thesis before draft
A documentary isn't a summary of a topic. It's an argument about one. Before writing a word, I identify the central tension — the thing that makes an audience feel the stakes personally.
03
Structure built for retention
Good hooks. Escalating stakes. Deliberate pacing. Every section earns the next. The goal is a script where the editor has to fight the urge to keep watching while cutting.
